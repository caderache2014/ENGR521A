WHAT THIS SCRIPT IS
-------------------
A Julia/SciML reproduction of Wang, Garnier & Rea (2023), "Hybridizing
Physics and Neural ODEs for Predicting Plasma Inductance Dynamics in
Tokamak Fusion Reactors" (NeurIPS Climate Change AI workshop). Their
RomeroNNV model: keep the two exact equations of Romero (2010) for
dLi/dt and dIp/dt, replace the third (V-dot) equation with a neural
network. Trained on 489 plasma discharges from MIT's Alcator C-Mod.

The reference implementation is in JAX/Equinox (Allen Wang shared a
code snippet). This script is a clean-room port to Julia/SciML.

  Closing the remaining gap looks structural, not compute-limited.

OPEN QUESTIONS (THINGS WE CAN'T SETTLE WITHOUT WANG'S HELP)
-----------------------------------------------------------
1. NORMALIZATION. Wang's RomeroNNV uses a quantity called `romero_norm`
   in his denominators. In his code it's a hard-coded machine constant
   from MachineConstants.for_cmod(). Our `liip_norm` is computed as
   max(|Ip|) * max(|Li|) over the dataset, ~3.66 in our scale.
   We do not know what value Wang's `romero_norm` actually evaluates
   to, and the difference could systematically shift NN gradients.

2. DATA PREPROCESSING. Wang's pipeline calls li_ip_pp.preprocess() on
   a pickle file before any training. The 489 CSVs Allen shared with
   us may be pre- or post-preprocessing -- we don't know. If post,
   we're fine. If pre, our training data differs from his evaluation
   data in subtle ways (filtering, smoothing, time-base alignment).

3. LOSS NORMALIZATION. Wang's LiIpLoss does jnp.sum(huber_loss(...))
   without per-shot or per-segment averaging. Our loss_multishot
   averages per segment then per epoch. For Huber the optima are
   identical, but effective gradient magnitudes differ. Probably
   minor but unverified.

4. NN OUTPUT BOUNDING. Wang uses unbounded MLP output (eqx.nn.MLP
   with no output activation). We had to bound to +/-5 after seeing
   solver instability with unbounded output (v58). It's possible Wang
   has a stability mechanism we're missing -- gradient clipping, weight
   decay, or something embedded in his Trainer class we don't have
   visibility into.

5. SHOT 1050218005 V-NN ARTIFACT. On our diagnostic shot, V_nn drifts
   smoothly to +0.6 by end-of-shot while real V drops to -2.34. Real V
   oscillates wildly; ours doesn't. This is the kind of behavior that
   would make Ip wrong because V appears in dIp/dt, but we can't tell
   if Wang's model has the same issue or not -- his paper only shows
   one example shot.
